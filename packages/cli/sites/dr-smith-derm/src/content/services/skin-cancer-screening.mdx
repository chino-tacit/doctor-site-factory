## Overview

Skin cancer is the most common cancer in the United States, but it's also one of the most treatable when caught early. Our board-certified dermatologists perform thorough full-body skin examinations using dermatoscopy for enhanced visualization.

## What We Screen For

- **Basal Cell Carcinoma** - Most common, rarely spreads
- **Squamous Cell Carcinoma** - Second most common, can spread if untreated
- **Melanoma** - Most dangerous, early detection is critical
- **Actinic Keratosis** - Precancerous lesions from sun damage
- **Merkel Cell Carcinoma** - Rare but aggressive

## The Screening Process

1. **History review** - Personal/family history, sun exposure, previous skin cancers
2. **Full-body examination** - Scalp to soles, including nails and mucosal surfaces
3. **Dermatoscopy** - Magnified, illuminated view of suspicious lesions
4. **Documentation** - Photography for baseline and monitoring
5. **Biopsy if needed** - Same-day for concerning lesions

## Who Should Get Screened

- Annual screening for everyone over 18
- Every 6 months if: personal history of skin cancer, family history of melanoma, immunosuppressed
- Any age with concerning moles or skin changes
